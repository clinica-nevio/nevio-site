Prophylaxis (hygiene)

What it is

Professional cleaning: scaling, airflow, polishing, plus personalized home-care guidance.

Who it’s for

Everyone — especially if you have sensitive gums, staining, tartar, or want effective prevention.

Typical duration

30–60 min (depending on build-up and sensitivity).

When to book

Every 6 months (or as advised), before whitening/cosmetic work, or if gums bleed.

Cosmetic dentistry

What it is

Improving appearance: whitening, veneers, aesthetic restorations, closing gaps.

Who it’s for

For stained, chipped, uneven teeth — with a goal of a natural-looking result.

Typical duration

Whitening: 45–90 min. Restorations: 45–120 min. Veneers: staged over multiple visits.

When to book

When you want a brighter, more harmonious smile. We typically recommend a hygiene visit first.

Endodontics (root canals)

What it is

Treating inflammation/infection inside the tooth and restoring it for function and comfort.

Who it’s for

For pain with hot/cold, pain when biting, deep decay, or trauma.

Typical duration

60–120 min, sometimes over 1–2 appointments depending on complexity.

When to book

Urgently if you have pain. For persistent symptoms, schedule a consult as soon as possible.

Implantology

What it is

Replacing missing teeth with a stable long-term option, planned individually.

Who it’s for

For missing teeth or teeth that cannot be saved. Options are confirmed after evaluation and imaging.

Typical duration

Consult: 30–45 min. Procedure: 60–120 min. Stages are defined in your plan.

When to book

As soon as you have a missing tooth — to prevent shifting and bite issues.

Orthodontics

What it is

Aligning teeth and correcting bite for aesthetics and function.

Who it’s for

For crowding, spacing, incorrect bite, or chewing discomfort.

Typical duration

Consult: 30–45 min. Treatment: from a few months to 18+ months depending on the case.

When to book

If you notice crowding or bite problems. Earlier is generally better.

Pediatric dentistry

What it is

Prevention, education, and age-appropriate treatments at a gentle pace.

Who it’s for

For first visits, routine check-ups, decay, or minor dental trauma.

Typical duration

20–45 min depending on cooperation and complexity.

When to book

Ideally early for acclimation. If pain appears, schedule promptly.
